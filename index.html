Russia following up with Telegram ban


More than one week ago

Recently, Russia took popular messaging app Telegram to court because of it's end to end encryption and refusal to provide encryption keys to the Russian government. The app goes against a Russian law allowing the government to spy on citizens, and because of Telegram's unwillingless to provide the encryption keys, it has now officially been banned in Russia. According to The Guardian, Russia has now blocked millions of IP address's attempting to enforce the ban on the Telegram app. This is just the first of many steps the government appears to be taking in order to get the Telegram app banned. The Russian government has also now requested to Google and Apple to remove the app from their app stores to help enforce their ban. It has also sent a request to popular site APKMirror, a site that allows you to download any app on the Google Play Store, that is well trusted in the community. Neither Google nor Apple has responded, but here's to hoping that they deny the request of banning Telegram.
